## 🛰️ Mars Rover AI: Algorithm Documentation

### 1. Mission Parameters & Time Management

The AI operates on a round-based system where **2 rounds = 1 hour**.

* **Day/Night Cycle:** The AI tracks `total_rounds % 48`. The first 32 rounds (16 hours) are **Day**, and the following 16 rounds (8 hours) are **Night**.
* **Solar Charging:** During the Day, the rover gains **+10.0 battery** per round. This is added *after* the action cost is calculated.
* **Endgame Mode:** Triggered when the mission has $\leq 2$ rounds remaining. The AI ignores safety thresholds to maximize final points.

### 2. Decision Hierarchy (The "Brain")

In every round, the AI evaluates conditions in this specific order:

1. **Harvesting (DIGGING):** * **Condition:** If the current cell $(x, y)$ contains a diamond (value 2, 3, or 4) AND battery is $\geq 2.0$ (or it is endgame).
* **Cost:** $-2.0$ battery.


2. **Survival (STANDING):** * **Condition:** If it is Night AND battery is $< 19.0$ AND it is not the endgame.
* **Action:** The rover stays still to prevent depletion.
* **Cost:** $-1.0$ battery (static drain).


3. **Exploration (MOVING):** * **Condition:** If the first two conditions aren't met, it scans for the "Best Target."
* **Selection:** It uses a scanning radius of 6 units and selects the target with the highest **Efficiency Score**.
* **Cost:** $-2.0 \times (\text{speed}^2)$.



---

### 3. Target Scoring & Pathfinding

The AI doesn't just go to the closest diamond; it calculates which diamond is "worth the trip."

#### **Cluster Value Calculation**

The AI looks at the target diamond and its 8 immediate neighbors to calculate a **Cluster Score**:

* **Gold (G):** 3.0 points
* **Yellow (Y):** 2.0 points
* **Blue (B):** 1.0 point

#### **The Efficiency Formula**

The final target is chosen by maximizing:


$$Final\ Score = \frac{\text{Cluster Score}}{\text{Time Cost} \times \text{Energy Cost}}$$

#### **Pathfinding**

It uses a **BFS (Breadth-First Search)** algorithm supporting 8-directional movement. This ensures the AI finds the shortest path while avoiding walls (`#`).

### 4. Data Export (File: `ai_route.txt`)

This is the data your GUI parses. It is saved in a **CSV-style format** without headers. Every decision step appends a new line.

| Column | Data Type | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **X** | Integer | The X-coordinate of the rover after the action. |
| **Y** | Integer | The Y-coordinate of the rover after the action. |
| **State** | Integer | `0`: STANDING, `1`: MOVING, `2`: DIGGING |
| **Speed** | Integer | The speed used during that round (`0`, `1`, `2`, or `3`). |

**Example Output:**

```csv
10,12,1,2
11,13,1,2
12,14,2,0

```

*(Translation: Moved to 10,12 at speed 2 -> Moved to 11,13 at speed 2 -> Dug at 12,14 speed 0)*
